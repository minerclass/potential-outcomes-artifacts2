# Sources and Privacy

## Purpose

The Research Outcomes & Action Studio is a prospective public artifact. It helps readers explore what possible findings could mean, how evidence should constrain recommendations, and how communities might respond responsibly.

It does not report dissertation results.

## Public/private boundary

This repository can contain:

- prospective scenarios;
- conceptual and ecological maps;
- de-identified, approved public findings after the study;
- public-facing briefs and practitioner tools;
- decision rules and intervention-planning protocols;
- links to public scholarship.

This repository must not contain or collect:

- participant names or contact information;
- consent records;
- interview recordings or transcripts;
- raw survey responses or exports;
- recruitment logs;
- identifiable quotations;
- private analytic memos;
- sensitive student, staff, district, or research information;
- API keys, credentials, or private tokens.

## Local-only interaction

The studio has no backend, analytics, form submission, or network-based data collection. Practice-to-Policy review progress is stored only in the visitor's browser through `localStorage`.

## AI-use boundary

Public AI tools should not receive identifiable or sensitive participant data. Any AI-supported research workflow must follow the approved IRB procedures and the dissertation AI-use disclosure. Substantive coding, interpretation, integration, recommendation, and final authorship remain the researcher's responsibility.

## Publication checklist

Before publishing future findings in this studio:

1. Confirm that findings are final enough for public dissemination.
2. Remove or generalize details that could identify participants or institutions.
3. Distinguish evidence, interpretation, recommendation, and limitation.
4. State where findings diverge or remain uncertain.
5. Verify that recommendations are proportional to the evidence.
6. Confirm all public links and source citations.
7. Recheck the public page after GitHub Pages deployment.
